
## Competitor Analysis

#### Slotomania
- Lots of colorful lights and effects
- Utilizes imagery of a romanticized vegas to draw in user into feeling like they are in a prestigious casino
- Multiple game modes with different bonus style and special winning conditions
- Pity payouts, basically guaranteed wins after a certain number of spins
- Auto spin function to allow for autonomous play
- Visuals which show why you won the spin, multiple ways to win money to keep excitement throughout the spin
- Got a mega win early, try to hook user?
- Beginner bundles for coins that are extra cheap as well as special in game currency deals to keep user paying


#### Caesar Slots
- Very similar to first one, lots of gamemodes, flashy effects, similar imagery
- Offer free spins, show how much won but never how much lost to allow for mindless spinning
- Not much to say here almost identical to the first game


#### FreeSlots
- Much more amateur feel, less UI effects, more basic layout, quick to the gameplay
- Simpler games with less flashiness on win 
- Still has lots of modes and indicators to show why you won
- Ability to queue up mass numbers of spins
- Unique gamemodes still exist with different mechanics and possible combos per slot



#### General Takeaways/Needs
- Lots of effects, try to use imagery that utilizes the romantacized imagery of Las Vegas
- Hardcode early winnings and bonuses, early dopamine hit will get user to chase that high later
- Pity rewards can encourage a user to continue to spin until they hit the pity reward to get a feeling they at least got something out of it
- If a spin could be a mega hit based on the first couple slots slow down the last slot to build anticipation
- Need many game modes, themes around greek myths or legends or animals are a good idea for theming
- Flashy effects on win and nothing on loss, keep them chasing even minor wins
- Auto spin or batch spin functions
- Free spin rewards
- Multiple different in game currencies to get them to spend money on an otherwise free game
