# Slot Machine Mechanics Research

Notes about how slot machines work / jargon relating to slot machines.

## How Slot Machines Work

Slot machines usually have 3 or more reels, which each have a number of symbols. The combinations of symbols that pay out if you bet on them are called **paylines**.
Each spin is independent of other spins, making it impossible to make accurate predictions of the results-- the game is pure luck. Machines typically use
random number generators to determine the payline of the play, and the result is already determined as soon as the play button is clicked.

There are many different types of slot machines. Smaller buy-ins usually pay out more often, but it is possible to pay more for a chance at a bigger jackpot.
However, the odds always favour the house.

## Paylines

Paylines are the predetermined paths across reels where symbols must match that result in a payout. Classic slot machines used to have a single horizontal payline
across three reels, but modern machines have multiple directions and many different paylines, such as horizontal, vertical, diagonal, and even zigzag patterns.

**Fixed** paylines require players to bet on every line per spin, which maximizes the chance of a payout but also increases the cost, while **Adjustable** paylines let the player
choose how many lines to update.

## RTP (Return to Player)

RTP represents how much, theoretically, a slot machine will pay back over its lifetime. For example, if a slot machine has a 96% RTP, it is designed to return 
$96 for every $100 spent on it. However, this RTP is measured over many games, meaning that it can't really be used to guarantee individual results, but rather give
insight to the long-term results of the machine.

## Volatility

Volatility measures a machine's risk by analyzing the frequency and size of payouts.

- **Low Volatility**: Frequent but smaller payouts, which helps allow players to play for longer
- **High Volatility**: Long periods of time without payouts, but huge payouts

## Wild Symbols

Wild symbols are similar to jokers in cards. They can typically substitute for any standard symbol to help the player win. For example, if a player needed 3
cherries to win, they could land 2 cherries and a wild symbol and still win.

## Scatter Symbols

Scatter symbols are special symbols that can usually land anywhere on the slots (rather than following a specific payline) to activate their effect. They usually
trigger special effects like free spins or multipliers.
